Monkeys


Monkeys inhabit various habitats across the globe, depending on their species. Some live in tropical rainforests, while others can be found in savannas, mountains, and even urban areas. Here's a breakdown:
  1. Amazon Rainforest, Brazil
    • The Amazon Rainforest is home to numerous monkey species, including capuchins, howler monkeys, spider monkeys, squirrel monkeys, and marmosets.
    • Brazil, being the largest country in South America and hosting the majority of the Amazon, has a significant monkey population.
  2. Borneo, Indonesia & Malaysia
    • Borneo, the third-largest island in the world, is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including a wide variety of monkey species.
    • Proboscis monkeys, macaques, langurs, and gibbons are among the primates found in the lush forests of Borneo.
  3. Madagascar
    • Madagascar is famous for its unique flora and fauna, including a diverse array of lemurs, which are a type of primate found nowhere else on Earth.
    • Lemurs inhabit various habitats across the island, from rainforests to dry spiny forests.
  4. Uganda
    • Uganda, located in East Africa, is home to several primate species, particularly in its national parks and forested areas.
    • Kibale National Park, in particular, is known for its population of chimpanzees and various monkey species, such as red colobus monkeys and black-and-white colobus monkeys.
  5. India
    • India is home to a rich diversity of monkey species, with populations found throughout the country's forests, mountains, and urban areas.
    • Rhesus macaques and gray langurs are among the most commonly encountered monkeys in India, often found in temple complexes, cities, and rural areas.
